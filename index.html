Section 1: Backward

I was born the youngest child of four. As a small child I showed an interest in mathematics. My sister tought me algebra with chalk on the wallpaper by my bed when I was four, and instead of drawing animals or houses, I drew maps and patterns and time. I used to get distracted in math class by my own questions: how do multiplication tricks look in bases other than ten? What is zero times infinity? I would often get in trouble for arguing with my teachers.

Section 2: Outward

I have a history of joining things in which I don't belong. I was a non-riding member of the equestrian team, a non-muslim member of the Muslim Student Association, I joined the Rowing Team in college having no sports experience (do we really want to count my time not riding horses?) and I became a physics major without ever having taken a physics class. As it turns out I disliked physics, but the pattern here is that I say yes to experiences and learning new things, often at the cost of looking a little silly. It has always been worth it. And if I hadn't tried physics, I wouldn't have resurrected my love for mathematics, eventually earning a degree in it.

Section 3: Inward

The pandemic really flipped my life inside out. I am a self-described violent flailing extrovert, but being on immunosuppressants meant I had to stay home, alone, for months. Sometimes I framed this as a fascinating challenge, and others I couldn't help but feel lonely and anchorless. Still, I benefitted. Apparently emotions can be felt in the body? Also, fierce boundaries reduce stress in the long term, improving health and happiness. Meditating is hard but cool

Section 4: Forward

The changes I made became my launch pad. With my mental health mostly in order and a clear set of questions (How should I pay for my life? How should I keep myself safe? What do I love? What can I give?), serendipity struck. I get to learn to code, which it turns out I love. (Well, I'm working at loving the frontend.) I get to solve puzzles and challenge myself and learn everyday. I never want to stop.
